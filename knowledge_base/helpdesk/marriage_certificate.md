# Marriage Certificate

## Common Questions & Answers

### "Who needs it?"
Anyone legally married in India who needs proof for legal, visa, or financial reasons.

### "Can I apply online?"
Yes, marriage registration can be initiated online. Visit your state's registration portal.

### "Do both partners need to be present?"
Yes, typically both partners need to be present at the registrar's office for registration.

### "What is the processing time?"
Varies by state — from a few days to a few weeks.

### "What if the marriage is not registered?"
An unregistered marriage may cause issues with legal documents, visas, and property claims. Registration is strongly recommended.

### "What about surname change?"
A gazette notification or updated Aadhaar/PAN after marriage can be used for surname change.

### "Lost certificate – what to do?"
Apply for a duplicate marriage certificate at the registrar's office where you registered.

### "What can be fixed in the certificate?"
Name, date of marriage, address, and other details can be corrected with supporting documents.

### "Can corrections be done online?"
Some corrections may be done online. Check your state's registration portal.

### "What documents are needed for correction?"
Supporting proof documents such as Aadhaar, birth certificate, and an affidavit may be required.

## Required Documents

### For New Marriage Certificate
- **Identity Proof** of both partners: Aadhaar Card, PAN Card
- **Age Proof**: Birth Certificate, School Leaving Certificate
- **Address Proof**: Electricity Bill, Ration Card
- Passport-size photographs of both partners
- Wedding invitation card (if available)
- Two witnesses with identity proof

### For Update/Correction
- Supporting documents for the specific change (Name, Date, Address)
- Aadhaar Card of both partners
- Affidavit (if required by the registrar)
- Original marriage certificate
- Carry original documents and 1 Xerox copy of each
