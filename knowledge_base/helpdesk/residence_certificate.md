# Residence Certificate

## Keywords
residence certificate, रहिवासी प्रमाणपत्र, current address proof, Talathi residence, Tehsildar residence, school admission proof, scholarship address proof, rent agreement certificate

## What it is
A Residence Certificate confirms a person's **current address**. It is needed for school admissions, scholarship applications, ration card, caste certificate, and various government schemes. **Different from Domicile Certificate** — Residence proves where you currently live; Domicile proves permanent state residence.

## Eligibility
- Any resident of the area can apply.
- The applicant usually must be present in person for verification.
- Parents or guardians can apply on behalf of minors using their own documents.
- People in rented houses are eligible — submit rent agreement + landlord's documents + utility bills.
- Owning agricultural land is **not required**.

## Required Documents
- Aadhaar card (generally required; other ID proofs may also be accepted)
- Ration card
- Electricity bill / property tax receipt / rent agreement
- Other valid residential proof
- For families: documents of family members residing at the same address are usually accepted.

## Where to Apply
- **Online**: State portal (Aaple Sarkar / MahaOnline) — aaplesarkar.mahaonline.gov.in
- **Offline**: Talathi office, Tehsildar office, or Common Service Centre (CSC)
- Final approval is given by the **Tehsildar**, even when applied through CSC.
- If you live in another taluka/district, apply at the Tehsildar/Talathi office of your **place of residence**.

## Office Hours
- Talathi & Tehsildar: 10:00 AM – 5:30 PM (Mon-Fri)
- Lunch break: ~1:30 PM – 2:30 PM
- CSC: 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM (timings may vary)

## Fees & Timeline
- **Fee**: ₹50 – ₹100 (varies by state)
- **Time**: 7 to 15 working days
- **Validity**: Usually 1 year (state rules vary)
- **Format**: Issued as printed paper and digital download

## Verification Process
The **Talathi** checks address proof, may visit the residence in some cases for physical verification, and confirms local records. The **Tehsildar** is the approving authority who issues the certificate with seal and signature.

## Common Questions

### "Residence aur Domicile Certificate mein difference?"
Residence = current address. Domicile = permanent residence in the state. Use case is different.

### "Aadhaar zaroori hai?"
Haan, usually mandatory. Other ID proofs may also be accepted.

### "Talathi ghar pe verification ke liye aate hain?"
Some cases mein haan — physical verification done if needed.

### "Online apply kar sakti hu?"
Haan, state ke official portal se (Aaple Sarkar / MahaOnline).

### "Rented ghar mein rehti hu, certificate mil sakta hai?"
Haan — submit rent agreement, landlord ke documents, aur utility bills.

### "Application reject ho gayi to?"
Haan reject ho sakti hai agar documents incomplete ya galat hain. Sahi documents ke saath dobara apply kar sakte hain.

### "Kahaan submit karu?"
Schools, government offices, scholarship portals, aur kai application processes mein.

### "Lost ho gaya to duplicate milega?"
Haan, request pe duplicate reissue ho sakta hai.

### "Time kitna lagta hai?"
~7-15 working days, verification depend pe.

### "Validity kab tak?"
Usually 1 saal — state rules pe depend karta hai.
