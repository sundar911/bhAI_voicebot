# Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)

## Keywords
PMJDY, Jan Dhan, जन धन, zero balance account, RuPay card, basic savings account, BSBDA, no minimum balance, बैंक खाता

## Objective
Financial inclusion — every household has at least one basic bank account, with insurance and overdraft facility.

## Key Benefits
- **Zero-balance** savings account (BSBDA)
- **RuPay debit card** with built-in ₹2 lakh accident insurance (for accounts opened after 28-Aug-2018)
- **Overdraft** up to ₹10,000 (for eligible account holders, after 6 months of satisfactory operation)
- **Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT)**: Govt scheme payments come into this account
- **Mobile banking & passbook** included

## Eligibility
- Indian resident, age 10+
- No minimum deposit required to open

## Documents Required
- Aadhaar (preferred — single document is enough)
- Or any one Officially Valid Document: Voter ID, PAN, Passport, Driving License + address proof

If no documents: a "Small Account" can be opened with just self-attested photo and signature/thumbprint, valid for 12 months (extendable on submitting valid proof).

## Where to Apply
Any public sector bank, regional rural bank, or participating private bank. Also at Bank Mitra (Business Correspondents) in villages.

## Choosing a Bank — Practical Guidance for Mumbai Users

A generic *"किसी भी सरकारी bank में जाओ"* is not useful — the user has to physically visit a branch and pick up the passbook later, so proximity matters. **Before suggesting specific banks, the bot should ASK the user which locality / station they live or work near** (e.g. *"आप कहाँ रहते हो — कौन सा station सबसे पास है? या काम कहाँ करते हो — BC के पास कोई branch देखेंगे या MIDC के पास?"*). Once the locality is known, suggest 2-3 government banks with a known presence there.

### Major government / public-sector banks with wide Mumbai branch coverage
The user can walk into any branch of these to open a Jan Dhan or regular savings account:
- **State Bank of India (SBI)** — largest branch network in Mumbai
- **Bank of Maharashtra** — strong presence in Maharashtra, often the first choice for Maharashtrians
- **Bank of Baroda** — wide Mumbai presence including Andheri, Bombay Central, Dadar
- **Bank of India** — strong in Mumbai, especially Andheri / Bandra / suburbs
- **Canara Bank** — good Mumbai coverage
- **Union Bank of India** — good coverage in central + suburban Mumbai
- **Central Bank of India** — older branches in BC / Grant Road / Marine Lines area

For BC area (Bombay Central / Grant Road / Tardeo): SBI and Bank of Maharashtra both have prominent branches near the station.
For MIDC area (Andheri East / Marol / Sakinaka): SBI and Bank of Baroda have multiple branches; the Marol area specifically has SBI branches near the industrial estate.

The bot should NOT invent specific branch street addresses. Instead, share the bank name + general locality (*"SBI की Grant Road के पास branch है"*) and let the user confirm via Google Maps or by walking in. Saying *"SBI MG Road branch, Khar West"* with a specific address you're not sure of risks sending the user to a wrong location.

### Documents needed (recap, for the bot's first reply)
- Aadhaar is enough for Jan Dhan (preferred)
- Or any one valid ID + address proof for a regular savings account
- Mobile number (for OTP / mobile banking)
- 1-2 passport-size photos (most banks ask)
- PAN is required only if depositing > ₹50,000 OR for regular savings accounts at most banks
