# ESIC (Employee State Insurance Corporation)

## General Information

### "How does ESIC work?"
Both employers and employees contribute to the ESIC fund. It provides medical, financial, and social security benefits to employees and their families.

### "Who is eligible?"
Employees earning up to ₹21,000/month (₹25,000 if disabled). Employers must register these employees.

### "What are the main ESIC benefits?"
- Free medical care for employees and their families
- Sickness benefit: 70% of wages for up to 91 days
- Maternity leave with pay
- Disability compensation (temporary or permanent)
- Support for family if employee dies due to work injury
- Unemployment allowance under Rajiv Gandhi Shramik Kalyan Yojana

### "What is the ESIC wage limit?"
₹21,000/month for regular ESIC coverage. ₹25,000/month for employees with disabilities. If salary exceeds the limit, no new coverage unless already enrolled.

### "Is ESIC mandatory?"
Yes. Mandatory for employees in establishments with 10 or more people, earning ₹21,000 or less per month.

## Registration

### "How does employee enrollment work?"
1. Once the employer is registered, they enroll eligible employees
2. Employees share personal info, Aadhaar, and family details
3. The system creates a 10-digit Insurance Number (IP)
4. Employees get their e-Pehchan card to access ESIC benefits

### "What is the employee contribution?"
0.75% of gross salary. If you earn less than ₹176/day, you don't pay — but your employer still does.

### "What is the employer contribution?"
3.25% of employee's gross salary every month, paid into the ESIC fund.

### "What if the employer skips ESIC registration?"
- Fine of ₹10,000 or more
- 12% interest per year on unpaid contributions
- Legal trouble under ESIC Act, 1948
- Employer may have to pay employee medical bills themselves

## Benefits

### "What medical benefits does ESIC provide?"
- Free treatment at ESIC hospitals, dispensaries, and empanelled private hospitals
- Consultations, medicines, diagnostic tests, hospitalization at no cost
- Specialist and super-speciality treatment including surgery and ICU care
- Maternity and neonatal care
- Preventive healthcare (vaccinations, annual check-ups)

### "How to claim ESIC benefits?"
1. Go to your nearest ESIC hospital with your e-Pehchan card or ESIC number
2. Meet the ESIC doctor and get a medical certificate if needed
3. Submit the claim form through your employer, ESIC office, or online portal
4. Once verified, payment is sent to your bank

### "Can I claim benefits after leaving a job?"
Yes. You can claim unemployment benefits, medical care (limited time), and support for dependents. Submit the right forms within the claim time limit.

## Claims & Processes

### "What documents are needed for a claim?"
Depends on the claim type. Visit www.esic.gov.in for the full list.

### "How long do ESIC claims take?"
- **Sickness and Maternity Benefits**: ~15 days after document submission
- **Disability and Dependents' Benefits**: Up to 30 days (needs medical check)
- **Unemployment Benefits**: Usually 1-2 months (job history verification)

### "How to check claim status / what if rejected?"
Visit www.esic.gov.in and log in. If rejected, common causes:
- Missing/incomplete documents
- Info not matching employer's records
- Delay in submission

Fix and re-submit, or visit your nearest ESIC office for review.

## Online Processes

### "How to get e-Pehchan Card?"
1. Go to the ESIC IP Portal and log in with your insurance number
2. Click 'Print e-Pehchan Card'
3. Download and print
4. Carry it to any ESIC hospital

### "Forgot ESIC password?"
1. Go to ESIC login page → 'FORGOT PASSWORD'
2. Enter your ESIC number and registered email
3. Check email and follow the reset link

## ESIC FAQs

### "What is the child age limit for ESIC benefits?"
Up to 18 years of age.

### "How to avoid ESIC rejection?"
Make sure all details match Aadhaar, documents are clear, and KYC is fully completed.

### "How long is TIC valid?"
90 days from the date of registration.

### "What is TIC used for?"
TIC (Temporary Identity Card) is used to access ESIC medical benefits until the permanent Pehchan Card is issued.

### "What documents are needed for KYC?"
Aadhaar card, bank passbook or cancelled cheque, photo, PAN card.
