# Government Schemes (Yojanas)

## Financial Support Schemes

### Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY)
**Objective**: Affordable credit up to ₹20 lakhs for micro/small enterprises in non-farm sectors (manufacturing, trading, services, allied agriculture like dairy, poultry, beekeeping).

**Loan Categories**:
- Shishu: up to ₹50,000
- Kishore: ₹50,001 – ₹5 lakh
- Tarun: ₹5,00,001 – ₹10 lakh
- Tarun Plus: ₹10 lakh – ₹20 lakh (existing Tarun borrowers with good repayment history)

**Eligible Borrowers**: Individuals, Proprietors, Partnership Firms, Pvt. Ltd. or Public Companies. Must have good credit history and basic skill/experience.

**Where to Apply**: Public/Private Sector Banks, Regional Rural Banks, Cooperative Banks, MFIs, NBFCs, Small Finance Banks.

**Interest Rate**: As per bank norms (RBI guidelines). Shishu loans: processing fee usually waived. **No agents/facilitators authorized under PMMY.**

**Documents Required**:
- *For Shishu (up to ₹50,000)*: ID Proof (Aadhaar/PAN/Voter ID), Address Proof (utility bill/Aadhaar/passport), 2 passport photos, quotation of machinery, business registration (if any).
- *For Kishore/Tarun/Tarun Plus*: All above + 6-month bank statement, IT returns, balance sheets (if loan > ₹2 lakh), business plan, partnership deed/MoA (if applicable), net-worth statement.

### Atal Pension Yojana (APY)
**Purpose**: Government-backed pension for Indian citizens aged 18-40 who don't pay income tax. Provides old-age income security for unorganized sector workers.

**Eligibility**:
- Age: 18 to 40 years
- Must not be income tax-payer (as of Oct 1, 2022)
- Must have a savings bank account
- Contributions continue till age 60 via auto-debit (monthly/quarterly/half-yearly)

**Benefits at Age 60**:
- Guaranteed monthly pension: ₹1,000 to ₹5,000 (based on contribution)
- Same pension to spouse after subscriber's death
- Lump sum to nominee after death of both subscriber and spouse

**Tax Benefit**: Section 80CCD(1) deduction.

**Early Exit (Before 60)**: Only own contributions + net interest returned (after charges). No return of govt co-contribution.

**Death Before 60**:
- Option 1: Spouse continues APY account till subscriber would have been 60
- Option 2: Full corpus returned to spouse/nominee

**Grievances**: www.npscra.nsdl.co.in → Home → NPS-Lite → CGMS

### Sanjay Gandhi Niradhar Anudan Yojana (Maharashtra)
**Objective**: Monthly financial assistance for destitute Maharashtra residents with no livelihood.

**Eligibility**:
- Resident of Maharashtra, BPL category
- Women: 18-65 years; Men: 65+ years
- Includes: destitute widows, orphans, divorced women, disabled (40%+), transgender persons with no support, HIV/AIDS patients without income

**Benefits**:
- ₹600/month (single beneficiary)
- ₹900/month (family with 2+ beneficiaries)

**Documents Required**: Domicile certificate (Maharashtra), income certificate, age proof (Aadhaar/birth certificate), disability certificate (if applicable), death certificate (if widow/orphan), divorce papers (if divorced), ration card, bank passbook, passport-sized photos.

### Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)
**Objective**: Savings scheme for the education and future of girl children.

**Eligibility**:
- Girl child below 10 years at account opening
- Only one account per girl child
- Maximum 2 girl children per family (3 in case of twins)

**Key Benefits**:
- Interest rate ~8% (updated quarterly), tax-free under Section 80C (up to ₹1.5 lakh/year)
- Min deposit: ₹250/year; Max: ₹1.5 lakh/year
- Maturity: 21 years from opening or upon marriage after 18
- Partial withdrawal (50%) allowed after girl turns 18, for education

**Documents Required**: Birth certificate of girl, ID/address proof of parent (Aadhaar/PAN), passport-sized photo, SSY account opening form (post office or authorized banks).

## Health Schemes

### Ayushman Bharat – PM-JAY (Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana)
**Objective**: Universal health coverage for poor and vulnerable populations.

**Key Features**:
- Coverage: ₹5 lakh per family per year (family floater, no age/size cap)
- Cashless secondary and tertiary hospitalization
- Pre-existing conditions covered from Day 1
- Pre/post hospitalization (15 days)
- Surgery, ICU, diagnostics, implants, medicines, food
- Fully funded by Central & State Governments

**Eligibility**:
- *Rural*: Families with kuccha house, no adult (16-59), no adult male, disabled member without able-bodied adult, SC/ST households, landless labourers. Auto-included: destitute, manual scavengers, primitive tribal groups, bonded labourers.
- *Urban (occupation-based)*: Ragpickers, beggars, domestic workers, street vendors, sanitation workers, construction workers, **artisans**, drivers, mechanics, electricians.

**Documents Required**: Age & Identity Proof (Aadhaar/PAN), proof of address, contact details, caste certificate, income certificate, document proof of family status (joint/nuclear), Aadhaar.

### Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY)
**Objective**: Maternity benefits for pregnant and lactating women from disadvantaged backgrounds.

**Benefits**:
- *First child (any gender)*: ₹5,000 in 2 instalments
  - ₹3,000 on pregnancy registration + 1 ANC check-up
  - ₹2,000 after childbirth registration + immunization till 14 weeks
  - Additional ₹1,000 under Janani Suraksha Yojana = ~₹6,000 total
- *Second child (only if girl)*: ₹6,000 in one instalment

**Eligibility**:
- Woman aged 19+
- Pregnant or lactating, facing wage loss
- Apply within 270 days of childbirth
- Must belong to: SC/ST, Disabled (40%+), or BPL Card holder

**Documents Required**: Aadhaar (mapped to bank/post office), bank account details, eligibility proof, MCP/RCHI card, LMP date, ANC date, child birth certificate, child immunization details.

### Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Jan Arogya Yojana (MJPJAY) – Maharashtra
**Launched by**: Govt. of Maharashtra (Public Health Dept.)
**Effective**: 1st July 2024
**Coverage**: Entire population of Maharashtra

**Key Benefits**:
- Categories A, B, C, E: ₹5 lakh per family per year (floater basis)
- Category D (Road Accident Victims): ₹1 lakh per person per year

**Medical Coverage**:
- A/B/C/E: 1356 packages across 34 specialties
- D: 184 packages (road accident-related)
- Follow-up: 262 packages
- Govt hospital exclusive: 119 packages

**Cashless Package Includes**: Hospitalization (bed, nursing, doctor fees), oxygen, ICU, drugs, implants, blood transfusion, X-rays, tests, inpatient food, transport after discharge, body transport in case of death.

**Eligibility by Category**:
- A: Yellow/AAY/Annapurna/Orange ration cardholders
- B: White ration cardholders + Maharashtra domicile
- C: Students/orphans/women/elderly in Govt institutions, journalists, construction workers
- D: Road accident victims from outside Maharashtra/India
- E: Families from 865 villages near MH-Karnataka border (AAY, PHH, Annapurna)

**Valid ID Proofs**: Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, DL, Passport, College ID, Health Card, Bank Passbook with Photo, Sr. Citizen Card, Handicap Certificate, Govt-issued photo ID.

## Livelihood Schemes

### Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana – NRLM (DAY-NRLM)
**Mission**: Reduce rural poverty by enabling poor households to access self-employment and skilled wage employment through grassroots institutions.

**Key Features**:
- Target: 7 crore rural poor households across 600 districts and 6 lakh villages in 8-10 years
- Community-led, demand-driven approach
- Intensive blocks: full implementation; non-intensive: limited support

**Core Components**:
- Social Mobilization & Capacity Building
- Financial Inclusion
- Livelihood Promotion
- Convergence with government schemes

**Key Benefits**:
- One woman from each rural poor household linked to Self-Help Group (SHG)
- SHGs get bank linkages, interest subvention, credit support
- Village-level federations support SHGs
- Special focus on single women, disabled, landless
- Revolving Fund to SHGs, Community Investment Fund (CIF) to federations
- Skill training & placements through RSETIs
- Insurance (life/health) and market access support

**Eligibility for SHGs**:
- At least 6 months of active existence
- Must follow Panchasutras (regular meetings, savings, inter-loaning, timely repayment, updated books)
- Must meet NABARD grading norms

**Documents Required**: Aadhaar number, proof of identity, proof of residence, Voter ID, passport-size photo.

### Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana – Urban (PMAY-U)
**Overview**: Subsidized pucca houses for urban poor under "Housing for All by 2022".

**Key Features**:
- Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS) for home loans
- Aadhaar mandatory for all beneficiaries

**Components**:
- *EWS (Income ≤ ₹3L)*: House ≤ 30 sq.m
- *LIG (₹3-6L)*: House ≤ 60 sq.m
- *MIG I (₹6-12L)*: House ≤ 160 sq.m
- *MIG II (₹12-18L)*: House ≤ 200 sq.m
- Slum Rehabilitation through private developers
- Beneficiary-Led Construction/Enhancement for EWS

**Eligibility**:
- Belongs to EWS/LIG/MIG I/MIG II
- No pucca house owned by any family member across India
- Resides in PMAY-covered town/city
- No prior govt housing scheme availed
- Family = Husband + Wife + Unmarried Children

**Required Documents**: Aadhaar, income proof (affidavit/certificate), ID & address proof (PAN/Voter ID), EWS/LIG/MIG certificate, salary slips/IT returns, bank details, property documents, affidavit of not owning pucca house.
